Navigation Bar
A Navigation Bar is a vital part of graphical user interface (GUI) that is used to assist the user in finding a specific location. They are usually found at the top of the page or at the side of the page depending on the theme of the website. The navigation bar at the top of the There are two types of Navigation Bar, the primary and the secondary. Usually, the primary navigation bar will be focused on navigating the website through and out of the main page. On the other hand, secondary navigation bar would be focused on navigating on one category of the website. Suppose if you're on a sports website and you want to be able to find a certain baseball bat. The primary navigation would have a list of sports whether it be football, soccer, hockey, baseball, rugby, et cetera. You would immediately click on the baseball button and be redirected to the baseball part of the website. There is a high chance there is a secondary navigation bar which would lead you to what is the subject relating to baseball you're looking for. You would click on the baseball bat or equipments and there would be a dropdown menu in the secondary navigation bar. You have arrived at your destination.
Site Map
A site map is best described as an organized spread out diagram for a of a website content information where we want to be able to look for. It goes by explaining how a user will travel through a website whilst clickong on URL and A site map can come in many different forms. It can either be in a textual form or a visual form. A text form is shown to us in form of what URL is shown avaiable and what is not in the site map. Text form has a huge advantage, they are best used to find broken links. On the other hand, the visual form shows us a diagram in layton's terms of how you get to one place from another.
